## SECTION 5: VERTICAL SLICES, NOT HORIZONTAL LAYERS

### The Wrong Way

"We spent 4 weeks on the database, then 4 weeks on the API, then ran out of time for the UI."

This is how projects fail. You build impressive infrastructure that no one can see or use.

### The Right Way

"Sprint 1: A user can register and see an empty dashboard. It's ugly but it works."

Build **one complete feature** before starting the next.

### The Test

Can a human use it? 

If yes, it counts. If no, it doesn'tâ€”no matter how good the code is.

### Why This Matters

- **Proves integration works** before you're committed
- **Creates visible progress** that maintains team morale
- **Enables feedback** from design partners and users
- **Reduces risk** by failing small and early

A working ugly thing beats a beautiful unfinished thing every time.

## SECTION 6: ESCALATION IS NOT SNITCHING

### Reframe How You Think About This

If a teammate disappears for a week, tell the instructor immediately.

This feels wrong. It feels like betrayal. It isn't.

### Why You Must Escalate

**The instructor has resources you don't:**
- Accommodations for students with disabilities or crises
- Extensions for documented circumstances
- Connections to counseling and support services
- Authority to restructure team responsibilities

**You only have resentment.**

Covering for a struggling teammate doesn't help themâ€”it enables the problem to compound until it's unsolvable.

### The Reframe

Escalation is how help arrives. Silence is how problems become catastrophes.

The person who escalates is not a snitch. They're the person who cared enough to get help when help was needed.

## SECTION 7: THE TEAM CONTRACT

### Required By End of Week 1

Every team must complete and submit a team contract. This is not optional. This is the foundation.

### What the Contract Covers

**Communication Expectations:**
- Primary channel (Slack, Discord, Teams)
- Expected response time (hint: 24 hours maximum)
- Standing meeting times

**Work Expectations:**
- Minimum hours per week per person
- How you handle missed deadlines
- How you handle unresponsive teammates

**Escalation Agreement:**
When does the instructor get involved? Define this explicitly:
- Teammate unresponsive for more than X days
- Same problem appears in 2+ retrospectives
- Interpersonal conflict affecting the work

**Collaboration Commitment:**
Every member commits to the Collaboration Minimum every sprint.

### Why This Matters

"We didn't think we'd need rules" is how teams discover they needed rules after the damage is done.

Writing it down before conflict arises creates a shared reference point when conflict arrivesâ€”and conflict always arrives.

## SECTION 10: THE THREE THINGS THAT ACTUALLY MATTER

### If You Remember Nothing Else

**Be responsive.** Answer messages within 24 hours. Always.

**Be honest.** If you're stuck, say so. If you're overwhelmed, say so. If you're going to miss a deadline, say so BEFORE the deadline.

**Be kind.** Your teammates are stressed too. Assume good intent until proven otherwise.

Teams that do these three things usually succeed. Teams that don't, don't.
