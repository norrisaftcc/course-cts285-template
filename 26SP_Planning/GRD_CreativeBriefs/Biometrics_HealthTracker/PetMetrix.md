### Company Background: Founder Story

**Sarah Martinez** was a graphic designer living in Portland with her 7-year-old golden retriever, Murphy. Murphy was her constant companion—walked twice daily, played fetch every evening, perfectly healthy as far as she could tell. One Tuesday morning, Murphy seemed “a little tired” after their walk. Sarah thought maybe he’d played too hard at the dog park the day before. By Tuesday evening he ate dinner but seemed subdued. Wednesday morning he didn’t eat breakfast—the first time ever. Sarah started to worry. By Wednesday afternoon, Murphy was lethargic and breathing heavily. She rushed him to the emergency vet, terrified. The diagnosis devastated her: advanced congestive heart failure. The emergency vet explained that Murphy’s heart had been failing for weeks, possibly months, but golden retrievers are stoic—they hide pain and illness incredibly well. His heart rate had likely been elevated for weeks. His activity had probably been gradually declining, but so slowly Sarah didn’t notice the pattern. His sleep had probably been restless as fluid built up in his lungs, but she thought he was just dreaming. The vet said words that still haunt Sarah: **“If we’d caught this earlier—if you’d noticed his resting heart rate climbing, his activity gradually declining by 20%, his respiratory rate increasing at night—we could have started medication weeks ago and managed this. Now we’re in crisis mode.”** Murphy survived the crisis, but it cost $8,000 in emergency care, and his quality of life was permanently diminished. He went from a vibrant, active dog to a carefully managed heart patient. Sarah was devastated—not just by the diagnosis, but by the guilt. **“He was showing warning signs I couldn’t see. His heart was struggling, but he still wagged his tail when I came home. He was sleeping restlessly, but I just thought he was having active dreams. His walks were getting shorter, but I thought maybe he was just getting older at 7.”** Six months later, Sarah started sketching what would become PetMetrix. She thought about all the data humans track—heart rate, sleep, activity—and realized **pets can’t tell us when something’s wrong, but their bodies can**. Continuous biometric monitoring could catch the subtle changes—elevated heart rate, reduced activity, disrupted sleep, increased respiratory rate—before they become emergencies. Murphy now wears a PetMetrix collar sensor. His veterinarian has access to his real-time data through the vet portal. When his heart rate starts climbing or his activity drops, they adjust his medication proactively instead of waiting for another crisis. He’s lived three more good years—not because his heart failure is cured, but because they’re managing it with data instead of guessing. Sarah built PetMetrix because **“Murphy taught me that ‘seems fine’ isn’t enough. I want other pet owners to catch what I missed.”**

## PART 2: PRODUCT DEEP DIVE

### What PetMetrix Is

PetMetrix is a **medical-grade biometric monitoring platform** for dogs and cats that continuously tracks vital signs and behavior patterns to detect health problems early. The core product is a collar-mounted sensor that monitors heart rate, breathing, temperature, activity, and sleep 24/7, syncing data to an app that alerts owners to concerning changes and provides veterinarians with actionable health data. PetMetrix is a modular ecosystem—the collar sensor is the foundational product, with additional monitoring devices (smart bowls, litter sensors, implantable chips) available for comprehensive health tracking.

### Physical Components: Collar Sensor (Primary Product)

**Collar-Mounted Biometric Sensor Pod:** Lightweight device (under 1 oz) clips securely onto any existing dog or cat collar | Works for small cats (6+ lbs) through large dogs (150+ lbs) | **Medical-grade sensors** measure heart rate and heart rate variability (HRV - stress and health baseline), respiratory rate (breaths per minute, breathing patterns), core body temperature (fever detection, hypothermia alerts), 3-axis accelerometer for activity tracking (active, resting, sleeping, playing, movement patterns), sleep quality monitoring (restlessness, sleep cycle disruptions, duration) | **Additional tracking:** Scratching frequency (counted via motion patterns - allergies, fleas, skin conditions), vocalization detection (excessive barking/meowing patterns - anxiety, pain, cognitive decline), movement quality analysis (limping, favoring limbs, reduced mobility, gait changes) | Waterproof rating IP67 (can swim, bathe, play in rain) | Rechargeable lithium battery (7-10 day life depending on activity) | LED status indicator (charging, connectivity, alert status - can be disabled for night) | Secure clip mechanism (won’t fall off during play) | Hypoallergenic materials (medical-grade silicone and plastic) | Small form factor (1.5” x 1” x 0.5”) blends into collar | Available in black, grey, and subtle colors to match pet aesthetic

**Charging Dock:** Magnetic charging base (pet doesn’t wear sensor during charging - 2 hour full charge) | USB-C connection | LED charging indicator | Weighted base stays in place

**Smartphone App:** Works with owner’s existing smartphone (iOS 14+ / Android 10+) | No separate hub device needed | Collects data continuously via Bluetooth Low Energy | Syncs to cloud when phone in range | Works offline (stores data locally, syncs later) | Multi-pet support (monitor multiple pets from one app)

**Packaging:** Premium pet health packaging (clean, trustworthy, not toy-like) | Collar sensor + charging dock + USB-C cable + quick start guide + sizing guide for proper collar fit | Retail price $179 one-time purchase + $15/year subscription (includes app access, cloud storage, vet portal, unlimited health reports)

### PetMetrix Ecosystem (Future Products)

While students will focus creative work on the Collar Sensor, understanding the ecosystem shows strategic brand vision:

**Litter Box Sensor** ($69 add-on, cats only) - Tracks frequency, duration, timing of litter box use | Detects urinary issues, constipation, behavioral changes | Early indicator of kidney disease, diabetes, blockages

**Implantable Biometric Chip** ($299 + vet procedure, future premium option) - Veterinarian-implanted sensor for permanent monitoring | Same data as collar but never removed | Premium option for chronic condition pets, breeding animals, high-value pets

**All devices sync to one PetMetrix app | One subscription covers all devices for all pets | Veterinarian portal has access to all device data**

### Digital Components: The PetMetrix App

**Owner Dashboard (Simple, Glanceable):**

**Home Screen:** “Everything is normal” as default (green checkmark, reassuring) | Current vital signs if clicked (heart rate, temp, activity today) | Recent alerts/notifications (if any) | Quick access to individual pets (multi-pet households) | Battery status for sensors

**Health Monitoring Views:**

**Heart Rate Tracking:** Current heart rate | Resting heart rate baseline (established over first 2 weeks) | **Heart rate variability (HRV)** trends (stress indicator, health marker) | Alerts for sustained elevated heart rate (not just momentary spike during play) | Breed and age-adjusted normal ranges | Historical trends (is resting HR climbing over weeks/months?)

**Respiratory Monitoring:** Current breathing rate | Sleeping respiratory rate (more stable, better baseline) | Alerts for elevated breathing (breathing harder at rest = lung/heart issues) | Pattern detection (irregular breathing, panting when shouldn’t be) | Historical trends

**Temperature Tracking:** Current body temperature | Normal range for this pet (established baseline) | **Fever alerts** (sustained temp above 103°F dogs, 103.5°F cats) | **Hypothermia alerts** (temp below 99°F - shock, illness) | Environmental context (is pet outside in heat/cold?)

**Activity & Sleep Analysis:** Today’s activity level (active, moderate, resting, sleeping) | Activity trends over time (gradual decline indicates illness, pain, aging issues) | **Sleep quality scores** (restless vs. deep sleep, frequent waking, sleep cycle disruptions) | Comparison to baseline (“20% less active than usual over past 3 days”) | Play patterns (helps identify pain - “used to play 30 min/day, now only 10 min”)

**Behavior Pattern Detection:**

**Movement Quality:** Limping detection (asymmetric movement patterns) | Reduced mobility (trouble standing, climbing stairs, jumping) | Gait changes (arthritis, injury indicators) | Shake-off frequency (after lying down - pain indicator in dogs)

**Scratching/Grooming:** Scratching frequency count (allergies, fleas, skin conditions, anxiety) | Location patterns if detectable (ear scratching vs. body) | Excessive grooming in cats (stress, skin issues, pain)

**Vocalization Patterns:** Barking frequency and duration (separation anxiety, cognitive decline, pain) | Nighttime vocalization (cognitive dysfunction in senior pets, pain) | Yowling in cats (pain, cognitive issues, hyperthyroidism)

**Alert System (Smart, Not Annoying):**

PetMetrix doesn’t alert for every minor fluctuation (that creates alert fatigue and anxiety). Instead, it identifies **meaningful patterns**:

**Immediate Alerts (Potential Emergency):**

- Sustained fever (temp >103°F for 2+ hours)
- Severe hypothermia (temp <97°F)
- Extremely elevated heart rate at rest (breed/age adjusted)
- Respiratory distress pattern (rapid breathing at rest)

**Concerning Pattern Alerts (Call Vet Soon):**

- Heart rate elevated for multiple days (not just during exciting events)
- Activity declined 30%+ over 3-5 days (early illness, pain)
- Sleep restless 3+ nights in a row (pain, breathing issues, anxiety)
- Scratching frequency 3x normal baseline (allergies, parasites, skin infection)
- Weight change detected (if using smart bowl - diabetes, thyroid, illness)

**Trend Notifications (Monitor, Discuss at Next Vet Visit):**

- Gradual activity decline over weeks (arthritis developing, aging, weight gain)
- Resting heart rate slowly climbing (heart disease developing)
- Sleep patterns changing (senior cognitive changes)

**Alert Format:** Push notification with specific data | “Murphy’s heart rate has been elevated (92 BPM vs. baseline 68 BPM) for 6 hours. Consider calling your vet.” | Not just “heart rate high” but context and guidance

### Veterinarian Integration (Key Differentiator)

**Vet Portal Access:**

With owner permission, veterinarians can access **real-time or historical PetMetrix data** through secure vet portal:

**Pre-Appointment Review:** Owner sends 30/60/90-day health report before appointment | Vet reviews data in advance, comes to appointment informed | Saves appointment time (no “has he been acting normal?” guessing)

**Baseline Health Profile:** Vet sees **this specific pet’s normal** (not just breed averages) | Makes deviations obvious (“Murphy’s normal resting HR is 68, today it’s 89—that’s significant for him even if within breed range”)

**Post-Surgery Monitoring:** After surgery or illness, vet monitors recovery remotely | Catches complications early (infection = fever + elevated HR, pain = activity decline) | Reduces need for in-person rechecks

**Chronic Condition Management:** For pets with heart disease, diabetes, arthritis, etc., vet tracks condition progression with real data | Adjusts medications based on actual vital signs, not owner’s “he seems okay” | Early warning when condition worsening (adjust treatment before crisis)

**Exportable Reports:** Generate PDF reports for vet appointments | Shows trends, baselines, recent alerts | Medical-standard formatting (vets take it seriously) | Can be added to pet’s medical record

**Direct Vet Alerts (Premium Feature):** Vet can set custom alert thresholds for their patients | “Alert me if Murphy’s heart rate >85 BPM sustained” | Useful for high-risk pets, post-operative monitoring

### How Physical + Digital Work Together

The collar sensor measures vital signs and behavior continuously (heart rate and HRV indicate stress and health baseline, breathing patterns reveal respiratory issues, temperature detects fever or hypothermia, movement analysis identifies pain or mobility changes, sleep quality shows overall wellness). Data syncs to the owner’s smartphone via Bluetooth where the app analyzes patterns, comparing to breed/age norms and this pet’s individual baseline established over the first two weeks. When concerning patterns emerge, the owner receives alerts with specific information and guidance (monitor at home, schedule vet appointment, seek emergency care). Veterinarians can access full data history through the secure vet portal, using objective metrics to diagnose and manage conditions rather than relying on owner’s subjective observations of whether the pet “seems okay.” The system catches gradual changes invisible in day-to-day observation (activity declining 30% over weeks, resting heart rate climbing, sleep becoming more restless) and identifies patterns that indicate specific health issues (sustained fever suggests infection, reduced activity plus movement asymmetry indicates pain or injury, increased respiratory rate at night signals breathing problems).

**Fitness Trackers for Pets (FitBark, PitPat):** Track activity and sleep (like Fitbit for pets) | Gamify pet fitness (“did your dog hit their step goal?”) | **No medical-grade biometric sensors** | Can’t detect fever, elevated heart rate, breathing issues | Not designed for vet integration or medical use | PetMetrix: “We’re medical-grade monitoring, not fitness gamification.”

**Smart Collars (existing competitors):** Some emerging products claim health monitoring | Most are GPS + activity with basic “wellness scores” | **Lack veterinary integration** (data not formatted for vets, no vet portal) | No established baselines (can’t tell what’s normal for YOUR pet vs. breed average) | Focus on consumer convenience, not medical accuracy | PetMetrix: “Vets actually trust and use our data—it’s designed for them, not just owners.”

**Owner Observation:** Pets hide pain and illness (evolutionary survival instinct) | Changes are gradual and easy to miss day-to-day | Owner’s description is subjective (“he seems fine,” “a little tired maybe”) | Vet appointments are infrequent (annual checkup misses 364 days of data) | By the time symptoms are obvious, condition is often advanced | PetMetrix: “Your pet can’t tell you—but their body can. **PetMetrix is the first platform that combines medical-grade biometric monitoring with veterinary integration.** It’s not about finding lost pets (GPS), gamifying fitness (step counters), or replacing vet visits (telemedicine). It’s about giving vets **objective, continuous data** about their patients so they can catch problems early, manage chronic conditions with real metrics, and make informed decisions. Peace of mind for owners, actionable data for vets.

### User Story 1: Jessica, 32, Marketing Manager with Senior Dog

Jessica makes $75K, lives in Seattle, has a 10-year-old beagle named Cooper. Cooper has always been healthy, but he’s slowing down—she assumed it was normal aging. Over the past 6 months, their walks got shorter (used to walk 2 miles, now does 1 mile and seems tired). He sleeps more (used to greet her at the door, now sometimes stays on the couch). He’s less interested in playing with his favorite squeaky toy. Jessica mentioned it to her vet at Cooper’s annual checkup: “He’s slowing down a bit, but he’s 10, right? That’s normal?” Vet said “Keep an eye on it, but yes, senior dogs slow down.” Three weeks after that appointment, Cooper suddenly couldn’t stand up one morning. Emergency vet visit: severe arthritis in his hips and spine, causing chronic pain. Vet said he’d probably been in pain for months, but beagles are stoic. By the time it was obvious, he was in severe pain. Now on medication, but Jessica feels awful: **“He was suffering and I thought he was just getting old. I noticed changes but didn’t realize they added up to a real problem.”** With PetMetrix, the gradual activity decline (down 35% over 6 months), reduced play behavior (toy interaction down 60%), changes in movement patterns (slower to stand, favoring back legs), and disrupted sleep (restless, waking frequently - pain indicator) would have been tracked and graphed. By month 3, PetMetrix would alert: “Cooper’s activity declining steadily, movement quality reduced, sleep restless. Pattern suggests pain or mobility issue. Discuss with vet.” Jessica could have shown vet objective data at the annual checkup, started pain management months earlier, before Cooper’s quality of life declined so much.

**Design takeaway:** Gradual changes are invisible day-to-day but obvious when graphed over time—owners need visual data showing patterns they can’t see living with pet daily.

### Final Reminders

This is not fill-in-the-blank design work. You’re creating a complete brand system for a medical device that could save pets’ lives by catching health problems before they become emergencies. Your interpretation of PetMetrix’s personality, visual identity, and market positioning is where the creativity happens. Avoid pet product clichés (overly cute, paw prints everywhere, baby-talk language, stock photos of obviously healthy puppies/kittens). PetMetrix is medical-grade health monitoring with emotional warmth—it’s serious technology driven by deep love for pets. The unique value proposition is **catching invisible health problems early through continuous biometric monitoring and giving vets actionable data instead of owner observations**—this should drive every design decision. Consider how the product serves two distinct audiences (pet owners who love their pets emotionally, veterinarians who need clinical data) and how design addresses both without confusing either. Pet owners need reassurance without anxiety. Veterinarians need credibility without complexity. Both need to trust the technology. Every deliverable should either build emotional connection with pet owners OR establish medical credibility with vets (or ideally both). This is portfolio-worthy work that demonstrates understanding of dual-audience design, medical device positioning, data visualization for non-technical audiences, building trust in health technology, and balancing emotion with evidence.

**Keep asking yourself:** Does this design build trust with someone whose pet is their family member? Does this messaging feel reassuring (we’ll help you protect your pet) or anxiety-inducing (your pet might be sick right now)? Would a veterinarian take this data seriously or dismiss it as consumer gadget? Does this differentiate PetMetrix from GPS trackers (health monitoring, not location) clearly? Would a pet owner understand what biometric monitoring is and why it matters? Does this balance sophisticated medical technology with approachable consumer product? Would someone trust their beloved pet’s health to this product? Does this respect that pet illness and loss are deeply emotional topics—not opportunities for fear-mongering? Does the design work equally well for dogs and cats (avoiding dog-centric bias)? Does this feel like it could actually save a pet’s life by catching problems early—not just collect interesting data?
